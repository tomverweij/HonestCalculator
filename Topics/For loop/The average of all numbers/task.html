The average of all numbers

Write a program that reads 2 numbers, a and b, from the input. As an output, it shows the mean of all numbers that lie on the interval between a and b, and are divisible by 3.

What does it mean? Let's say, you need to calculate the mean of the numbers in the range \( [-5; 12] \). The numbers divided by 3 without the remainder are: \(-3, 0, 3, 6, 9, 12\). Their sum is \(27\), there are six of them, so the mean is \(27 \div 6 = 4.5\).

The input interval always contains at least one dividend of 3. Remember to include the values of a and b in your calculations.

Tip: To calculate the mean, you need to get the sum of the found integers and divide it by the number of these found integers. To get the divider, consider using a separate variable for counting the number of found integers: it should have 0 as the starting value, then 1 should be added to it each time you find an integer that can be divided by 3.


Sample Input:
-5
12

Sample Output:
4.5



Memory limit: 256 MB
Time limit: 5 seconds
